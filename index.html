A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam was a renowned aerospace engineer and played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was often referred to as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to the country's defense and space technology.

biographies

  • Birth: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Education: Kalam studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, Chennai. He graduated in 1954.
  • Election: In 2002, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan.
  • Passing: Unfortunately, Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, after suffering a heart attack while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
  • Inspiration: Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam is remembered as the "Missile Man of India" and an inspirational figure, particularly for the youth. His speeches and writings continue to motivate people to strive for excellence.
